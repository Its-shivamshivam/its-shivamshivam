Research

Research Interests

My investigations are centered upon nonlinear continuum mechanics, finite deformation kinematics, constitutive modeling, and experimentally informed damage characterization of advanced fiber-reinforced laminated composite systems. The principal emphasis of my research lies in the formulation of rigorous multiscale frameworks capable of describing complex deformation processes, constituent-level damage evolution, and geometric incompatibilities arising in composite materials subjected to finite strain regimes.

Core Themes of My Research

Finite Deformation Mechanics

My work employs multicontinuum theory proposed by Bedford and Stern together with the theory of multiple natural configurations developed by Rajagopal and Srinivasa.

Composite Materials

The framework is applicable to both stiff composite systems such as CFRP and GFRP laminates and soft architected systems.

Damage Mechanics

Damage evolution is characterized through matrix cracking, fiber breakage, interfacial slip, debonding, and delamination.

Experimental Investigation

Experimental studies involve tensile testing, mixed-mode bending experiments, and progressive damage characterization.

Damage Characterization

Matrix Cracking

\[ \mathbf{G}_{m} = \frac{1}{J_{m}^{d}} \mathbf{F}_{m}^{d} \left( \mathrm{Curl} \, \mathbf{F}_{m}^{d} \right) \]

Matrix incompatibility corresponding to crack evolution.

Fiber Breakage

\[ \mathbf{G}_{f} = \frac{1}{J_{f}^{d}} \mathbf{F}_{f}^{d} \left( \mathrm{Curl} \, \mathbf{F}_{f}^{d} \right) \]

Fiber incompatibility governing rupture evolution.

Geometric Interpretation

Matrix Configuration Torsion

\[ T^{D}_{mAB} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \Gamma^{D}_{BA} - \Gamma^{D}_{AB} \right) \neq 0 \]

Geometric incompatibility associated with matrix damage.

Fiber Configuration Torsion

\[ T^{D}_{fAB} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \Gamma^{D}_{BA} - \Gamma^{D}_{AB} \right) \neq 0 \]

Fiber torsion interpretation corresponding to fiber breakage.
