Research

Research Interests

My research integrates advanced theoretical mechanics, computational formulations, and experimental investigations to understand nonlinear constitutive behavior, damage evolution, and failure mechanisms in fiber-reinforced laminated composites under finite deformation regimes.

Theoretical Mechanics

Nonlinear constitutive modeling and finite deformation.

Numerical Simulation

Computational frameworks for composite systems.

Experimental Investigation

Tensile tests, MMB tests, and validation studies.

Research Areas

Core Themes of My Work

Finite Deformation Mechanics

Development of advanced constitutive frameworks using multicontinuum theory and multiple natural configuration concepts proposed by Rajagopal and Srinivasa.

\[ \mathbf{F}=\mathbf{F}^{e}\mathbf{F}^{r}_{\alpha}\mathbf{F}^{d}_{\alpha} \]

Composite Materials

Modeling of both stiff and soft fiber-reinforced composites including CFRP, GFRP, origami structures, and biological composite systems.

Damage Mechanics

Characterization of matrix cracking, fiber breakage, interfacial slip, debonding, and delamination using configurational incompatibility measures.

\[ \mathbf{G}_{m}=\frac{1}{J_{m}^{d}}\mathbf{F}_{m}^{d}(\mathrm{Curl}\,\mathbf{F}_{m}^{d}) \]
