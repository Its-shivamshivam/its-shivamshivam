Research

Research Interests

My research endeavors are situated at the intersection of theoretical mechanics, finite deformation kinematics, constitutive modeling, and experimental mechanics of advanced composite materials. The principal objective of my work is the rigorous characterization of nonlinear deformation and damage evolution in fiber-reinforced laminated composites subjected to finite strain regimes. My investigations integrate multicontinuum theory, differential geometric formulations, computational mechanics, and experimentally validated constitutive frameworks.

Core Themes of My Research

Finite Deformation Mechanics

My research utilizes multicontinuum theory together with multiple natural configuration theory for constitutive modeling of nonlinear composite systems under large strain deformation regimes.

\[ \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{F}^{e} \mathbf{F}^{r}_{\alpha} \mathbf{F}^{d}_{\alpha} \]
\[ \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{F}^{e} \mathbf{F}^{i} \]

Composite Materials

The proposed framework is applicable to both stiff and soft composite systems including CFRP, GFRP, biological tissues, and origami-inspired architected composites.

Damage Mechanics

My work characterizes matrix cracking, fiber breakage, interfacial slip, debonding, and delamination using geometric incompatibility and constituent-level deformation measures.

Experimental Investigation

Experimental investigations involve tensile testing, mixed-mode bending (MMB) tests, and damage evolution characterization in advanced laminated composites.

Damage Characterization

Constituent-level damage evolution is characterized through finite deformation kinematics, incompatibility measures, and multicontinuum constitutive descriptors.

Matrix Cracking

\[ \mathbf{G}_{m} = \frac{1}{J_{m}^{d}} \mathbf{F}_{m}^{d} \left( \mathrm{Curl} \, \mathbf{F}_{m}^{d} \right) = J^{-1}_{m} \mathbf{F}^{d}_{m} \, \mathrm{curl} \left( \mathbf{F}^{d-1}_{m} \right) \]

Matrix cracking evolution is quantified through the incompatibility tensor associated with matrix deformation.

Fiber Breakage

\[ \mathbf{G}_{f} = \frac{1}{J_{f}^{d}} \mathbf{F}_{f}^{d} \left( \mathrm{Curl} \, \mathbf{F}_{f}^{d} \right) = J^{-1}_{f} \mathbf{F}^{d}_{f} \, \mathrm{curl} \left( \mathbf{F}^{d-1}_{f} \right) \]

Fiber rupture mechanisms are characterized through incompatibility measures associated with fiber deformation.

Interfacial Slip

\[ \lambda_{m} = \mathbf{F}^{d}_{m} \mathbf{V}_{rel} = \mathbf{v}^{d}_{m} - \mathbf{F}^{d}_{m} \mathbf{F}^{d-1}_{f} \mathbf{v}^{d}_{f} \]
\[ \mathbf{V}_{rel} = \mathbf{F}^{d-1}_{m} \mathbf{v}^{d}_{m} - \mathbf{F}^{d-1}_{f} \mathbf{v}^{d}_{f} \]

Relative constituent motion governs interfacial slip and debonding evolution in laminated composites.

Delamination

\[ \tilde{\Sigma} = \llbracket \mathbf{F}^{l} \rrbracket, \qquad \tilde{\Sigma}_{\delta} = \Sigma^{T} \vartheta^{l}_{\delta} = \sigma^{T} \vartheta^{e}_{\delta}, \qquad \delta = \xi,\eta \]

Delamination is represented through displacement discontinuity measures across the interface manifold.

Geometric Interpretation of Incompatibility

Differential geometric measures are employed to establish rigorous incompatibility interpretations associated with constituent-level damage mechanisms in finite deformation composite systems.

Matrix Configuration

\[ T^{D}_{mAB} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \Gamma^{D}_{BA} - \Gamma^{D}_{AB} \right) = \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{F}^{d-1}_{m} \left( \partial_{B}\mathbf{F}^{d}_{mA} - \partial_{A}\mathbf{F}^{d}_{mB} \right) \neq 0 \]

The torsion tensor associated with the matrix natural configuration provides the incompatibility measure corresponding to matrix crack evolution.

Fiber Configuration

\[ T^{D}_{fAB} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \Gamma^{D}_{BA} - \Gamma^{D}_{AB} \right) = \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{F}^{d-1}_{f} \left( \partial_{B}\mathbf{F}^{d}_{fA} - \partial_{A}\mathbf{F}^{d}_{fB} \right) \neq 0 \]

Fiber breakage is characterized through the torsion generated in the fiber natural configuration arising from local incompatibility.

Interface Contribution

\[ T^{D}_{IAB} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \mathbf{F}^{l-1} \right)^{D}_{a} \Delta S_{R} \left( n_{B} \llbracket F^{l} \rrbracket^{a}_{A} - n_{A} \llbracket F^{l} \rrbracket^{a}_{B} \right) \]

The interface incompatibility term represents the geometric measure corresponding to delamination and interfacial discontinuities.

Delamination Torsion Measure

\[ T^{D}_{AB} = h_{\xi} T^{D}_{\xi AB} + h_{\eta} T^{D}_{\eta AB} + T^{D}_{IAB} \]

The total torsion field incorporates bulk and interface incompatibility contributions to fully characterize delamination-induced geometric defects.
